What is the Future of Mental Health & Wellness?

What does that Future look like if MIT is in the leadership?

[Under Construction]
A New MIT Student Center Gallery
February 16 - March 2


10 Artists were challenged:

  • Could you move them to see Mental Health differently, to act differently, to want to know more?
  • Could you shape MIT’s investments in MIT-based research to make more informed decisions, for example?
  • And if you could say it, in the most MIT way possible, what would you put together? What would it look like?

how?

Ten Artists + Makers were chosen from the MIT community.

Working in the MIT Studio over IAP, they work together transforming their messages into captivating exhibits that will be on view for two weeks this February in a first floor Gallery of the MIT Student Center. It’s no coincidence— we love the MLK Design Studio model.

who?

MIT Students, Staff, Faculty, and Alums

Applicants were chosen on the basis of their potential contribution to an interesting, provoking, and diverse gallery experience. Artist. Hacker. Coder. Makers. Performer. Video Producer. Whatever you want to call yourself is great. We loosely define “Art” to be inclusive: What makes a captivating exhibit at MIT?

where?

We Curate. We Create. Together.

We meet once a week, in the SAA Art Studio. During the rest of the week artists continue working at their leisure in the art studio and makerspaces around campus. This a group project where each artist has their own individual exhibits, that live together for a week in the Gallery. On Thursdays, artists + makers and Instructors gather together for feedback on artist implementation.

Craft your message to MIT

MIT is a big place.

The Minds, Hands, and Hearts of our community are beautifully diverse. As such, no one approach to Mental Health and Wellness will resonate with everyone. That’s why we called for 10 Artists + Makers to speak up, through their exhibits, to send a message to their community:

We need to be together.
We need to give space.
We need to be trained.
We need to be intuitive.
We need to slow down.
We need to live like there’s no tomorrow.

We need to know more.
If you could send one message to MIT, what would it be?

"He who works with his hands is a laborer.
He who works with his hands and his head is a craftsman.
He who works with his hands and his head and his heart is an artist."
